A Lifeline: Job Opportunities

Even after constant governmental efforts to provide better job opportunities to acid attack survivors, the ground reality is that most of the Acid Attack Survivors are unemployed even after mastering some skills and struggle to find jobs. But, there are many organisations which are regularly hiring Acid Attack survivors and giving them equal job opportunities.



1. SODEXO INDIA :-

SODEXO INDIA has been an active hiring organisation for Acid Attack Survivors from last few years. It has placed survivors in frontline jobs across sectors. Not only this, SODEXO India is also contributing to Social Inclusion of the survivors by has sensitisation sessions for other employees.

Amidst COVID-19, they continuously work from home and are developing AI projects to tackle the world after COVID-19.

You can visit their career page from here.



2. Shoppers Stop :-

Shoppers Stop has been associated with two Delhi-based NGOs -- Make Love Not Scars and Chhanv Foundation which has worked with acid-attack victims and survivors for over a year now. It contributes Re. 1 on every bill generated for beauty products purchase towards this cause. Watch this short Youtube Video

Along with that, they hire Acid Attack Survivors as well for various roles like HR, Technical along with others sectors.

You can visit their career page from here.



3. Lemon Tree Hostels :-

Since the last 1 and a half years, Lemon Tree Hostels is hiring Acid Attack Survivors in all its 80 chains across India.They have been placed in housekeeping and reservations centres. It is expected that this group will hire even more survivors by the coming years soon.

Amidst COVID-19, when the hotel servies were halted, the employees were given 60% of their salaries and is working from home regarding their upcoming projects.

You can visit their career page from here.



4. Select CityWalk :-

SELECT CITYWALK is a shopping centre located in Saket, New Delhi. It promotes various campaigns including #BanAcidSale and provides a lot of opportunities to Acid Attack Survivors including Cashiers, Voice-over artists and modelling.

Amidst COVID-19, it provided various online training platforms for the survivors to develop their skills and regularly paid them for their jobs.

You can visit their career page from here.
