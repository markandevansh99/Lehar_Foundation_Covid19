Providing Free Medical Assistance to Survivors

It is stated in the pages of law that immediate medical care will be provided to the survivors free of cost by any hospital or clinic whether run by the Central Government, the State Government, Local Bodies or any other person

See our Survivor Rights page for more information



Now, after immediate medical care, the hospitals hardly provide any assistance to these survivors for their face reconstruction surguries. This is the reality. These surguries are of extreme importance as they act as a new lifeline & ray of hope for survivors to do something in life.

Recently, many NGOs and organisations run by Human Welfare Organisation along with some recovered survivors are aiming to provide free surgeries for acid attack and burn victims. From skin grafting to hair transplant and even critical reconstructive surgeries, these foundations and NGOs tries to finance all crucial surgeries and medical cost for the survivor.



1. ATIJEEVAN Society :-


Atijeevan Society is registered under The Society Act, who has been fighting for empowerment and rehabilitation of acid attack survivors for more than 5 years now. The organization is even going to guide them with Medical and Legal Assistance with the help of Team of Professionals and Donors. The primary aim is to help them in this lifelong battle, by supporting their rehabilitation physically, mentally and economically.

2. PCVC-International Foundation for Crime Prevention and Victim Care :-

The International Foundation for Crime Prevention and Victim Care (PCVC) was founded in 2001 in Chennai, Tamil Nadu to create and extend support services for women affected by domestic and interpersonal violence. Since the last 3 years, PCVC is regularly conducting projects where it provides free surgeries and face reconstruction support to about 100-200 survivors. They did a project in April and now, upcoming session is expected to be in July.
